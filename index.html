Method & reliability

Activation windows. Liquidations are counted only from the block each comet's feeds were switched to the SVR aggregator on-chain (Comet proxy Upgraded event): Ethereum cUSDTv3 2025-09-18, cUSDCv3 2025-10-23; Base cAEROv3 2026-02-03, cUSDCv3 2026-04-10; Arbitrum cUSDTv3 & cWETHv3 2026-02-12, cUSDCv3 2026-04-10. This removed 468 pre-activation liquidations (incl. 9 falsely credited as SVR on cUSDCv3 before its switch).

Liquidations & available incentive. Every AbsorbDebt event per comet (value liquidated = on-chain usdValue). Available OEV = Σ collateral_usd × storeFrontPriceFactor × (1 − liquidationFactor), read on-chain. Theoretical-max discount, so "leaked" is an upper bound.

Recapture (USD at recapture-time). Anchored to the verified 354.49 ETH delivered to the DAO recipient (gross ≈ 644 ETH at 55/45), allocated across SVR-routed mainnet liquidations by available incentive and valued at each month's ETH price. Per-row = estimate; the ETH total is exact. Mainnet routing is detected via same-block bids to the Flashbots collector 0x149B….

Base/Arbitrum — verified against Chainlink docs. Chainlink's docs confirm Atlas (the L2 SVR auction, contract 0x583dcFef…, v1.6.4) is live on Base, Arbitrum & BNB with Compound listed as a supported protocol — so SVR is operational there. But on Atlas the winning bundle is submitted by the Chainlink oracle network through the Atlas contract; the largest Compound L2 liquidations (a $428K Arbitrum wstETH liquidation, a $140K Base liquidation) were instead won by independent bots in the public mempool (their own contracts, no Atlas bundle, no bid paid), and the L2 recipient balances are static (1.43 ETH Base / 0.59 ETH Arbitrum, unchanged 90+ days). So Compound's L2 OEV is currently leaking to ordinary liquidators: SVR is switched on, but the Atlas auction isn't effectively capturing these markets (no/low searcher bidding on Compound's L2 feeds). L2 recapture is treated as ~0; the small static balances are not from these liquidations and couldn't be traced (L2 explorer doesn't index the recipient's inflows).
