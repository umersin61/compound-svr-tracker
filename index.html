Method & reliability

Comets. SVR feeds were onboarded on 8 comets: Ethereum cUSDCv3/cUSDTv3 (live since Oct/Sep 2025); Base cUSDCv3/cWETHv3/cAEROv3 and Arbitrum cUSDCv3/cUSDTv3/cWETHv3 (onboarded ~Dec 2025–2026; Arbitrum was pushed through despite the RFP vote not approving OEV there).

Liquidations & available incentive. Every AbsorbDebt event per comet (value liquidated = on-chain usdValue). Available OEV = Σ over seized collateral of collateral_usd × storeFrontPriceFactor × (1 − liquidationFactor), all read on-chain (storeFrontPriceFactor 0.60, 0.70 on WETH comets; liquidationFactors per asset). This is the theoretical-max discount, so "leaked/missed" is an upper bound (real liquidator profit is net of gas and competition). Fully reliable inputs.

SVR routing & recapture (ETH). A liquidation is SVR-routed when the Chainlink collector Safe 0x149B…14e5 received an ETH bid in the same block — this reconciles with Chainlink's reported SVR counts. The verified anchor is the 354.49 ETH delivered to Compound's DAO recipient (gross ≈ 644 ETH at 55/45). Per-row DAO/Chainlink ETH allocate that verified total across SVR-routed mainnet liquidations by available incentive; treat per-row figures as estimates, totals as exact. The collector is shared across Aave/Venus/Compound, so exact per-liquidation isolation isn't possible on-chain.

Base/Arbitrum. Liquidations are enumerated, but the collector shows only dust on those chains — no SVR recapture is flowing on-chain yet, so those rows carry available incentive but no recapture. Base cUSDCv3 and Arbitrum cUSDCv3 are capped at the 200 most-recent in-window liquidations (L2 log pagination). Most L2 liquidations are sub-dollar test absorbs.
